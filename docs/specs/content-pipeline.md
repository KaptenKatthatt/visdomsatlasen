# Content Pipeline

## Purpose

This document defines how content moves through Visdomsatlasen.

The goal is consistency.

Every room should follow the same editorial process regardless of who creates it.

The pipeline ensures that every published room meets the same standards of quality, accuracy and experience.

---

# Overview

Every room follows the same lifecycle.

Idea

↓

Research

↓

Outline

↓

Draft

↓

Review

↓

Revision

↓

Approval

↓

Publication

↓

Maintenance

No step should be skipped.

---

# Stage 1 — Idea

The process always begins with a human question.

Examples:

- What is courage?
- Why do we suffer?
- What is forgiveness?
- What makes a good life?

The question becomes the foundation for everything that follows.

Deliverable:

- Question
- Initial description
- Suggested theme

---

# Stage 2 — Research

Collect relevant source material.

Prioritize:

- Primary sources
- Reliable translations
- Historical context
- Multiple perspectives when appropriate

Research should be documented.

Deliverable:

- Source list
- Notes
- Bibliography

---

# Stage 3 — Outline

Create the structure of the room.

Suggested outline:

- Question
- Opening
- Core idea
- Reflection
- Historical context
- Sources
- Further reading

No prose should be written before the outline is approved.

Deliverable:

- Editorial outline

---

# Stage 4 — Draft

Write the first complete version.

The draft should follow:

- Manifest
- Experience
- Content Philosophy
- Editorial Guidelines

The goal is clarity, not perfection.

Deliverable:

- Draft v1

---

# Stage 5 — Review

Review the draft using the project checklists.

Required reviews:

- Language
- Sources
- Experience

Deliverable:

- Review comments

---

# Stage 6 — Revision

Revise the draft based on review.

Typical improvements include:

- removing unnecessary text
- simplifying language
- improving flow
- improving historical accuracy
- improving readability

Deliverable:

- Draft v2

---

# Stage 7 — Approval

A room should only be approved when it satisfies all project principles.

Approval confirms that:

- the room respects the source material
- the writing feels like Visdomsatlasen
- the experience is calm
- the room contributes something meaningful

Deliverable:

- Approved room

---

# Stage 8 — Publication

Publishing should be simple.

Publishing should never modify the content itself.

Publication only changes status.

Draft

↓

Review

↓

Published

↓

Archived

Publishing should be reversible.

---

# Stage 9 — Maintenance

Published rooms are living documents.

Updates may include:

- better translations
- improved historical understanding
- clearer writing
- additional sources
- corrected mistakes

Every revision should be tracked.

---

# Versioning

Every room should include:

- Version
- Created date
- Last updated
- Reviewer
- Source verification status

Previous versions should remain available through version control.

---

# AI Responsibilities

AI may:

- suggest questions
- collect sources
- summarize material
- generate outlines
- create drafts
- compare traditions
- suggest improvements

AI must never publish automatically.

---

# Human Responsibilities

Humans approve publication.

Humans resolve ambiguity.

Humans decide what belongs in Visdomsatlasen.

The final editorial responsibility always belongs to a human.

---

# Quality Gates

Every room must pass these gates:

✓ Editorial Review

↓

✓ Source Verification

↓

✓ Language Review

↓

✓ Experience Review

↓

✓ Publication

If one gate fails, the room returns to Revision.

---

# Design Philosophy

The pipeline exists to protect quality.

It is acceptable for rooms to move slowly.

It is never acceptable to lower editorial standards simply to publish more content.

Quality is the product.
