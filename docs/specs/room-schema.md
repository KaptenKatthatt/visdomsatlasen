# Room Schema

## Purpose

A room is the fundamental reading experience in Visdomsatlasen.

It is not an article.

It is not a lesson.

It is not a chapter.

A room explores one human question through carefully curated ideas, reflections and sources.

Every room follows the same structure to create a consistent experience.

---

# Principles

Every room should:

- explore exactly one human question
- have one clear central idea
- be readable in a few minutes
- encourage reflection
- support deeper exploration
- never overwhelm the reader

---

# Required Fields

## id

Unique identifier.

Example

room_forgiveness

---

## title

Human readable title.

Example

Forgiveness

---

## slug

URL friendly identifier.

Example

forgiveness

---

## question

The human question explored by the room.

Examples

What is forgiveness?

Why do people suffer?

How do we find courage?

---

## summary

One or two sentences introducing the room.

This appears in previews and search results.

---

## opening

A short introduction.

Purpose:

Invite reflection.

Not explanation.

Recommended length:

50–150 words.

---

## core

The heart of the room.

This is the primary reading experience.

Recommended length:

300–800 words.

---

## reflection

Open questions for the reader.

Never provide correct answers.

Recommended:

2–5 questions.

---

## takeaway

One sentence.

The thought the reader carries away.

Not a quotation.

Not a conclusion.

Simply one clear thought.

---

## historical_context

Optional.

Provides historical background when needed.

Should not dominate the room.

---

## sources

Primary sources used in the room.

Each source should include:

- title
- author
- tradition
- date (if known)
- location in source
- translation used

---

## related_questions

Questions closely connected to this room.

Examples

Forgiveness

↓

Justice

↓

Mercy

↓

Revenge

---

## tags

Simple descriptive tags.

Used for search only.

Never exposed as primary navigation.

---

## notes

Editorial notes.

Not visible to readers.

May include:

- research reminders
- review notes
- future improvements

---

# Optional Fields

## images

Historical illustrations.

Artwork.

Maps.

Manuscripts.

Images should always support understanding.

Never decoration.

---

## audio

Optional narrated version.

---

## pronunciation

Optional pronunciation guide for historical names.

---

## timeline

Optional historical timeline.

---

## glossary

Optional explanation of difficult concepts.

---

Allowed values:

Review

---

# Relationships

Every room belongs to one primary question.

A room may connect to many other questions.

Relationships are directional.

Example

Forgiveness

↓

Justice

↓

Mercy

↓

Reconciliation

---

# Reading Time

Target:

3–7 minutes

Maximum:

10 minutes

Longer material belongs in the Library.

---

# Writing Constraints

One room.

One question.

One central idea.

Everything else is optional.

---

# Editorial Rule

If removing a paragraph makes the room stronger,

remove it.

If adding a paragraph only explains what the reader already understands,

do not add it.

---

# Technical Rule

The room must remain independent.

A room should be renderable by:

- web
- mobile
- tablet
- e-reader
- PDF
- future platforms

without changing its content.
